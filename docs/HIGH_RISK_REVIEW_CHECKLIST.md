# High-Risk Food Entries Requiring Veterinary Review

This document lists the PetPilot food entries that must be manually verified against authoritative veterinary sources before the site is considered reliable for emergency or clinical guidance. These items either carry a high risk of severe toxicity, have narrow safety margins, or are commonly involved in accidental poisonings.

## Recommended Review Process

For each high-risk entry:

1. **Cross-check with authoritative sources:**
   - [ASPCA Animal Poison Control](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control)
   - [Pet Poison Helpline](https://www.petpoisonhelpline.com/)
   - [AVMA Poison Guide](https://www.avma.org/resources/pet-owners/petcare/poisonous-items-animals)

2. **Verify the following fields:**
   - `safety.dogs.status` and `safety.cats.status`
   - `safety.dogs.severity` and `safety.cats.severity`
   - `symptoms` (include earliest and most dangerous signs)
   - `symptoms_severity` (map symptoms to severity levels)
   - `what_to_do` (direct, accurate emergency guidance)
   - `requires_emergency_visit` (true when professional care is needed)
   - `safe_amount` (avoid overstating safety; use conservative language)
   - `dosage_per_weight` (remove if not supported by toxicology data)
   - `notes_for_puppies` and `notes_for_kittens` (young animals are more vulnerable)

3. **Flag uncertain data.** If a field cannot be confidently verified, mark it as needing review rather than filling it with plausible-sounding text.

4. **Add or update sources.** Every high-risk entry should cite at least one authoritative veterinary source with a working URL.

5. **Have a veterinarian review.** Ideally, a licensed DVM should audit Priority 1 entries before the site is used by the public.

## Notes

- The current dataset was generated programmatically with AI assistance. It provides a useful starting point but is **not guaranteed to be accurate** for every entry.
- "Safe" items can still cause individual adverse reactions. Always advise users to introduce new foods slowly and watch for signs of intolerance.
- When in doubt, direct users to call a poison control hotline or their veterinarian rather than relying solely on the website.
