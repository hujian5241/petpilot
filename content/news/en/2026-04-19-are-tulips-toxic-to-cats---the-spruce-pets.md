## What happened
The Spruce Pets examined whether tulips are toxic to cats as spring gardening begins.

## Key facts
Tulips contain allergenic lactones and glycosides, most concentrated in the bulb. Chewing leaves, stems, or bulbs can cause oral irritation, drooling, vomiting, diarrhea, and depression. Bulb ingestion can lead to more serious illness.

## What pet owners should do
Prevent cats from accessing bulbs and cut flowers, clean up fallen plant material, and call a veterinarian if any part of a tulip is eaten.
