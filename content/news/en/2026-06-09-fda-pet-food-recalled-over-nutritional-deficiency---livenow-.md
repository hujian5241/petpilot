## What happened
Go Raw expanded a recall of its Steve’s Real Food freeze-dried dog and cat food after regulators, including the FDA, warned that the products may be deficient in a key vitamin. The recall now covers products distributed in roughly 20 U.S. states, with some reports citing more than 400,000 pounds of affected food. Most outlets identify the missing nutrient as vitamin B1 (thiamine), which is essential for nerve function.

## Key facts
- **Brand/Product:** Go Raw / Steve’s Real Food freeze-dried dog and cat food.
- **Reason for recall:** Potential low thiamine (vitamin B1) levels; some reports also mention vitamin D.
- **Scope:** Expanded to about 20 states; more than 400,000 pounds of product reportedly affected.
- **Risk:** Thiamine deficiency can cause neurological problems such as weakness, tremors, loss of coordination, seizures, and death in severe cases.
- **Affected animals:** Dogs and cats.

## What pet owners should do
- Stop feeding any recalled products immediately and keep them away from pets.
- Compare package lot numbers, UPCs, and best-by dates against the official FDA or manufacturer recall list.
- Watch for neurological symptoms and seek veterinary care right away if they appear.
- Return or safely discard the food according to the recall instructions; do not rely on appearance or smell.
