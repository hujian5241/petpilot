categories:

tags:

safety:
  dogs:
    status: toxic
    severity: critical
    summary: >-
      Metaldehyde-based snail baits are highly toxic to dogs; ingestion can
      cause rapid-onset tremors, seizures, and hyperthermia. Iron phosphate
      formulations are generally less toxic but may still cause gastrointestinal
      upset.
  cats:
    status: toxic
    severity: high
    summary: >-
      Metaldehyde-based snail baits are toxic to cats and can cause neurological
      signs such as tremors and seizures. Iron phosphate products are less toxic
      but may cause vomiting or diarrhea.
symptoms:
  - tremors
  - muscle rigidity
  - seizures
  - hyperthermia
  - excessive salivation
  - vomiting
  - diarrhea
  - ataxia
  - rapid breathing
  - weakness
  - coma
what_to_do: >-
  If your pet ate snail bait, contact your veterinarian or a pet poison control
  center immediately. Do not wait for symptoms. Bring the product packaging. If
  ingestion was very recent and a professional instructs you, you may be asked
  to induce vomiting.
requires_emergency_visit: true

active_ingredients:
  - metaldehyde
  - iron phosphate
