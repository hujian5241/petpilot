# Sunflower Seeds

Shelled, plain sunflower seeds are safe for dogs in small amounts. They provide healthy fats and vitamin E. Salted or flavored seeds should be avoided.

## Why Sunflower Seeds Matter for Pets

Sunflower seeds are a convenient, crunchy snack that many people keep on hand. Dogs may be attracted to their smell and texture, and some owners wonder whether they can share a few. The answer depends largely on preparation: plain, shelled seeds are very different from salted, flavored, or in-shell varieties.

## How Sunflower Seeds Affects Dogs and Cats

Plain sunflower seeds contain healthy fats, vitamin E, and a small amount of protein. In moderation, they are unlikely to harm most dogs. However, the fat content means they should be limited, especially for pets prone to pancreatitis or weight gain.

Salted seeds can contribute to excessive sodium intake, which may lead to thirst, vomiting, or more serious problems in pets with heart or kidney disease. Flavored coatings often include garlic, onion powder, or other ingredients that should be avoided.

Cats can eat a few plain seeds, but seeds are not a natural part of their carnivore diet and provide little benefit.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog eats a small handful of plain, shelled seeds.
- A pet gets into a bag of salted or flavored sunflower seeds.
- A dog swallows sharp seed shells along with the kernels.
- A well-meaning owner adds too many seeds to homemade treats.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, excessive thirst, or signs of abdominal discomfort. If your pet ate shells or a large amount of salted seeds, contact your veterinarian for guidance.

## Safer Alternatives

Pumpkin seeds, carrots, and green beans offer crunch and nutrients with fewer risks. Keep these on hand for guilt-free treat sharing.
