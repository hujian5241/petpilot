# Alcohol

Alcohol, also called ethanol, is far more toxic to pets than to humans because of their smaller size and metabolism. It is found in beer, wine, cocktails, mouthwash, certain desserts, and even unbaked bread dough. Signs of intoxication can appear within an hour and progress quickly. Any suspected alcohol ingestion in a pet should be treated as an emergency.

## Why Pets Cannot Tolerate Alcohol

A single ounce of liquor or a few laps of wine can overwhelm a small dog or cat. Pets lack the enzymes needed to break down ethanol efficiently, so it stays in their system longer and reaches higher concentrations. What seems like a minor indulgence to a human can be fatal to a pet.

## How Ethanol Affects the Body

Alcohol is a depressant that slows brain function. Pets become disoriented, wobbly, and sleepy. Their breathing slows and their body temperature drops. Blood sugar can fall to dangerous levels, causing seizures or collapse. In severe cases, the pet may fall into a coma and stop breathing.

## Hidden Sources in the Home

Alcohol is not limited to drinks. Mouthwash, cough syrup, hand sanitizer, vanilla extract, perfume, and cleaning products all contain ethanol. Desserts like tiramisu, fruitcake, chocolate liqueurs, and rum balls can also deliver a toxic dose. Unbaked yeast dough is another hazard because it ferments in the warm stomach and produces alcohol.

## Cats Face Extra Risk

Cats are small and fastidious groomers. If they walk through a spilled drink, they will lick the alcohol off their paws and fur. Because of their low body weight, even a tiny amount can cause symptoms. Any spill where a cat may have walked should be cleaned immediately.

## What to Expect at the Vet

Treatment focuses on supportive care. The vet may induce vomiting if the ingestion was recent and the pet is alert. Intravenous fluids help flush alcohol from the body and stabilize blood pressure. Warmth, oxygen, and medications to control seizures or acid-base imbalances may be needed. Most pets recover if treated early.

## Prevention Is Simple

Never leave alcoholic drinks within reach of pets. Clean spills right away, especially if cats are present. Store mouthwash, medications, and cleaning products in closed cabinets. Keep desserts containing alcohol out of reach, and do not let pets near unbaked dough.

## Safe Treat Alternatives

For a special treat, offer plain water, a small amount of low-sodium broth without onion or garlic, or a few pet-safe fruits and vegetables. There is no safe amount of alcohol for pets, so complete avoidance is the only rule.
