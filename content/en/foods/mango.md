# Mango

Mango flesh is safe for dogs in moderation, but the large pit can cause choking or intestinal blockage.

## Why Mango Matters for Pets

Mango is a popular summer fruit, and it is natural to want to share a sweet bite with your dog. The good news is that the flesh is not toxic. The concerns are the large pit, the tough skin, and the high sugar content. With proper preparation, mango can be an occasional treat for healthy dogs.

## How Mango Affects Dogs and Cats

The soft, juicy flesh of a ripe mango is easy for most dogs to chew and digest in small amounts. The sugar and fiber can cause diarrhea if too much is given at once. The pit is the most dangerous part because it can block the airway or intestines. The skin is less dangerous but can irritate the mouth and is hard to digest.

## Common Scenarios

A common scenario is a dog getting a few small pieces of peeled, pitted mango as a treat. This is generally fine for healthy dogs. A more serious situation is a dog grabbing a whole mango and chewing or swallowing the pit. Dried mango strips are also popular but often contain added sugar and are more concentrated than fresh fruit.

## What to Watch For

After your pet eats mango, watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or signs of abdominal discomfort. If you suspect the pit was swallowed, contact your veterinarian immediately. Signs of blockage include repeated vomiting, inability to keep food down, lethargy, and a painful abdomen. Pets with diabetes or weight issues should avoid mango or only have it on a vet's advice.

## Safer Alternatives

Blueberries and bananas are lower-risk fruit options for dogs. For cats, small pieces of plain cooked meat or commercial cat treats are more appropriate than fruit. If you want a hydrating treat for a dog, cucumber slices or small pieces of watermelon without seeds can be good choices.
