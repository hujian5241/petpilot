# Cranberries

Plain cranberries are safe for dogs in small amounts. Avoid sweetened cranberry products.

## Why Cranberries Matter for Pets

Cranberries are famous for their potential role in urinary tract health, which makes them an appealing snack for pet owners. The good news is that plain, unsweetened cranberries are safe for dogs in small amounts. The problem comes with cranberry sauces, juices, and sweetened dried cranberries, which often contain sugar and other ingredients that are not good for pets.

## How Cranberries Affect Dogs and Cats

Fresh cranberries are tart and low in sugar compared to many fruits. They contain fiber and compounds that may support urinary health. However, too many cranberries can cause stomach upset or diarrhea because of the acidity and fiber. Sweetened versions add calories and may contain toxic ingredients like raisins or xylitol.

## Common Scenarios

A safe scenario is a dog receiving a few fresh cranberries or a small amount of unsweetened dried cranberries. A risky scenario is a pet getting into cranberry sauce during a holiday meal or drinking cranberry juice. Some owners give cranberries hoping to prevent urinary tract infections, but this should not replace veterinary care.

## What to Watch For

After feeding cranberries, watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or loss of appetite. If your pet ate a large amount of sweetened cranberry product, contact your veterinarian. Pets with diabetes, obesity, or urinary issues should only have cranberries on a vet's advice.

## Safer Alternatives

Blueberries and raspberries are similar tart fruits that many dogs enjoy. For urinary health, ask your veterinarian about appropriate supplements or prescription diets. For cats, meat-based treats are the best choice. If you want to make homemade treats, use unsweetened dried cranberries sparingly and avoid all added sugars.
