# Watermelon

Seedless watermelon flesh is a hydrating, low-calorie treat for dogs. Avoid seeds and the tough green rind.

## Why Watermelon Matters for Pets

Watermelon can be a welcome treat on hot days because it is mostly water and low in calories. It offers a sweet flavor that many dogs love, and it can be a better alternative to high-fat or sugary snacks. When prepared safely, it fits easily into a balanced treat plan.

## How Watermelon Affects Dogs and Cats

Plain, seedless watermelon flesh is generally gentle on a dog's stomach in small amounts. The high water content can support hydration, but the natural sugars mean it should be limited. Cats may try a small piece, though they usually prefer savory foods and do not need fruit.

## Common Scenarios

- Offering seedless cubes during a summer walk.
- A dog grabs a rind or seeded slice from the trash.
- A pet licks watermelon-flavored candy or drink.
- Frozen cubes are given as a cooling enrichment activity.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or signs of blockage if rind or seeds were eaten. A large amount of fruit sugar can also cause loose stool. Contact your vet if your pet seems uncomfortable or cannot keep food down.

## Safer Alternatives

Blueberries, cucumber slices, or small apple pieces can provide similar hydration and crunch without the risks of seeds and rind. Always serve them plain and in moderation.
