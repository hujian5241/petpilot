# Walnuts

Plain English walnuts are not as dangerous as black walnuts, but they are high in fat and can cause gastrointestinal upset. The biggest concern is mold, which can produce tremorgenic mycotoxins leading to seizures.

Cats should avoid walnuts due to high fat content and the risk of mold. Safer treats include carrots and blueberries.

## Why Walnuts Matter for Pets

Walnuts are calorie-dense and can go rancid or moldy quickly. The mold that grows on walnuts can produce toxins that affect the nervous system. Black walnuts are especially dangerous and should never be fed to pets.

## How Walnuts Affect Dogs and Cats

Fresh English walnuts may cause vomiting or diarrhea if a pet eats too many. Moldy walnuts can lead to tremors, weakness, and seizures. The high fat content also increases the risk of pancreatitis.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog finds walnuts that have fallen from a tree.
- A bowl of walnuts is left on a table or counter.
- A pet eats walnuts from trail mix or baked goods.
- Moldy or old walnuts are discovered in a pantry.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, weakness, tremors, or seizures. If you suspect moldy walnut ingestion or see neurological signs, contact your vet or an emergency clinic immediately. Also seek help if your pet swallowed walnut shells.

## Safer Alternatives

Offer crunchy vegetables like carrots or sweet treats like blueberries. These foods are lower in fat and free from mold risk. Keep all nuts, especially walnuts, in sealed containers out of reach.
