# Strawberries

Strawberries are safe for dogs in small amounts. They contain fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants. Avoid sugary strawberry products.

## Why Strawberries Matter for Pets

Strawberries can be a fun, low-calorie treat that many dogs enjoy. They offer a small boost of vitamins and antioxidants, and their high water content can be refreshing on warm days. When fed correctly, they fit well into a balanced treat plan.

## How Strawberries Affect Dogs and Cats

For dogs, a few plain strawberries are usually easy to digest. The fiber and moisture can support healthy digestion, while the natural sugars are generally harmless in small amounts. Cats may accept a tiny taste, but their digestive systems are designed for meat, so fruit should never become a regular part of their diet.

## Common Scenarios

- Sharing a fresh berry with your dog after washing it.
- A puppy eats a strawberry with the green top still on.
- A pet licks strawberry syrup or dessert off a plate.
- Frozen berries are offered as a cool summer snack.

## What to Watch For

Watch for soft stool, diarrhea, or mild stomach upset, especially after a large portion. Strawberry stems and leaves are not highly toxic, but they can be tough on the stomach. Sugary strawberry products can lead to weight gain and dental problems.

## Safer Alternatives

If your pet loves fruit, consider blueberries, raspberries, or small apple slices without seeds. These options are also low in calories and easy to serve in controlled portions.
