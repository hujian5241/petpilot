# Celery

Celery is a crunchy, hydrating snack that is very low in calories. It can be a good treat for dogs on a weight-loss plan. The stringy fibers can be difficult to digest, so cutting celery into small pieces is recommended.

## Why Celery Matters for Pets

Celery is a staple in many kitchens and a common snack for people trying to eat lighter. For pets, it offers a satisfying crunch without many calories. It can be especially useful for dogs that need to lose weight or for owners who want a non-greasy training treat.

## How Celery Affects Dogs and Cats

Celery is mostly water and fiber, which makes it hydrating and low in calories. The crunch may help with oral stimulation, though it should not replace dental care. Some dogs enjoy celery as a satisfying alternative to high-calorie biscuits.

Cats can eat a tiny piece, but most are indifferent. Because cats are obligate carnivores, celery offers little nutritional value for them.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog on a weight-loss plan gets celery as a crunchy reward.
- A pet owner shares plain raw celery from a snack plate.
- A cat shows curiosity and nibbles a small piece.
- A dog eats celery that fell from a soup or stew containing onions and salt.

## What to Watch For

Cut celery into small pieces and remove stringy fibers to reduce choking and digestive upset. Avoid celery from soups, stews, or salads that contain onions, garlic, salt, or heavy dressings.

## Safer Alternatives

Carrots, cucumber slices, and green beans provide similar crunch and hydration. These are all good low-calorie choices for dogs.
