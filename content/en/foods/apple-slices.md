# Apple Slices

Apple slices without seeds or core are a crunchy, low-calorie treat for dogs. They provide fiber and vitamins A and C.

## Why Apple Slices Matter for Pets

Apples are easy to find, affordable, and naturally sweet, which makes them a tempting snack to share. For dogs, they can be a refreshing alternative to high-calorie biscuits. The key is preparation: removing the core and seeds turns a potentially risky fruit into a safe, nutritious treat. For cats, apples are not a dietary necessity, but a tiny piece is generally harmless if prepared correctly.

## How Apple Slices Affect Dogs and Cats

The flesh of an apple is mostly water and fiber, which can support digestion in small amounts. Chewing apple slices may also help clean teeth by gently scraping plaque. However, the seeds contain cyanogenic glycosides that can release small amounts of cyanide when chewed. The core is firm and can cause choking, especially in small dogs or greedy eaters. Cats lack the taste receptors for sweetness and rarely benefit from fruit, so any apple should be offered only as an occasional curiosity.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog begs for a bite of your apple while you are snacking.
- You want a healthy training treat for a weight-sensitive dog.
- A puppy finds a whole apple on the ground and eats the core.
- You consider adding apple to a homemade dog treat recipe.
- Your cat shows interest in the texture or smell of sliced apple.
- You buy commercial apple chips and wonder if they are pet-safe.

## What to Watch For

Most dogs tolerate apple slices well. Watch for signs of digestive upset such as vomiting, diarrhea, or decreased appetite, especially after a larger portion. If your pet swallowed seeds or the core, contact your veterinarian if you notice difficulty breathing, weakness, drooling, or abdominal pain. Routine chewing difficulty or coughing after eating may indicate a piece became lodged in the throat.

## Safer Alternatives

Blueberries, carrots, and plain pear slices are similarly low-calorie choices. For pets with sensitive stomachs, small pieces of cooked pumpkin or banana may be easier to tolerate. If you want a crunchy dental reward, consider vet-approved dental chews instead of relying on apples alone.
