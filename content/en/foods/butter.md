# Butter

Butter is not toxic to dogs or cats, but it is almost entirely fat. Eating butter can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and in some cases, pancreatitis, a painful and potentially serious inflammation of the pancreas.

A tiny accidental lick is usually not an emergency, but butter should not be offered as a treat. Pets with sensitive stomachs, obesity, or a history of pancreatitis should avoid butter entirely.

## Why Butter Matters for Pets

Butter is one of the most common fatty foods pets get into. Because it smells appealing and melts easily, dogs may lick a dish clean or eat an entire stick. That concentration of saturated fat can overwhelm the digestive system and lead to pancreatitis.

## How Butter Affects Dogs and Cats

The high fat content in butter can trigger vomiting and diarrhea. In some pets, especially those prone to pancreatitis, it can cause abdominal pain, lethargy, and loss of appetite. Dairy fat may also cause loose stools in lactose-intolerant animals.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog licks butter from a plate, counter, or mixing bowl.
- Butter is used to hide medication or coat treats.
- A pet eats baked goods that are high in butter.
- An entire stick of butter is left within reach.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, decreased appetite, abdominal pain, or lethargy. If your pet seems uncomfortable, is repeatedly vomiting, or has a history of pancreatitis, contact your vet promptly.

## Safer Alternatives

For hiding pills, try plain canned pumpkin, a small amount of plain yogurt, or a vet-approved pill pocket. For treats, offer carrots, green beans, or apple slices. These options are lower in fat and easier on the stomach.
