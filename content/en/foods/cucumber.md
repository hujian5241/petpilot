# Cucumber

Cucumbers are an excellent low-calorie treat, especially for overweight dogs. They are composed mostly of water, making them hydrating on hot days. Cats may ignore cucumber or accept a small piece.

Never feed pickles, which contain salt, vinegar, and spices that can upset a pet's stomach. Plain raw cucumber is best. Cut it into small pieces to prevent choking, and supervise your pet while eating.

## Why Cucumber Matters for Pets

Cucumber is a simple, hydrating snack that fits well into a weight-management plan. It adds crunch and volume without adding many calories, making it ideal for pets that beg for treats. Many dogs enjoy the fresh texture, especially on warm days.

## How Cucumber Affects Dogs and Cats

Dogs digest cucumber easily when it is cut into small pieces. The high water content can help with hydration, while the fiber supports regular bowel movements. Cats do not require vegetables, but a tiny piece is not harmful if they show interest.

## Common Scenarios

- Replacing high-calorie biscuits with cucumber slices.
- A pet grabs a pickle or seasoned cucumber slice.
- Adding chilled cubes to a bowl on a hot afternoon.
- A cat bats at a slice and takes a curious bite.

## What to Watch For

Watch for mild diarrhea if a pet eats a large amount. Pickles and seasoned cucumbers can cause stomach upset due to salt and vinegar. Always supervise to prevent choking, especially in small dogs.

## Safer Alternatives

Carrot coins, celery sticks, or green beans are similar low-calorie options. They provide crunch and nutrients without excess calories or seasoning.
