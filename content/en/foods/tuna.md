# Tuna

Tuna is a fish many cats love, but it should be an occasional treat rather than a staple. Canned tuna packed in water and with no added salt is best. Tuna packed in oil or brine is too fatty or salty.

Long-term feeding can lead to mercury accumulation and nutritional imbalances. Avoid tuna intended for humans as a regular meal replacement.

## Why Tuna Matters for Pets

Tuna has a strong smell and taste that many pets find irresistible, especially cats. Because it is so appealing, owners may be tempted to offer it frequently. However, tuna does not provide complete nutrition, and overuse can lead to health problems. Treating tuna as an occasional snack rather than a dietary foundation keeps pets safer.

## How Tuna Affects Dogs and Cats

Small amounts of plain, water-packed tuna are generally safe for both dogs and cats. The main concerns are mercury content, sodium levels, and the risk of replacing balanced meals. Cats fed large amounts of tuna may develop a preference for it and refuse their regular cat food. Dogs may experience stomach upset from oily or seasoned tuna.

## Common Scenarios

- **Plain water-packed tuna:** A small spoonful as an occasional treat is usually fine.
- **Tuna packed in oil or brine:** Too fatty or salty; should be avoided.
- **Tuna salad with mayo or onions:** Unsafe for pets; do not share.
- **Tuna as a daily meal:** Can cause nutritional imbalances and mercury concerns.
- **Tuna juice:** Small amounts may be okay, but offer fresh water instead.

## What to Watch For

Signs of trouble include vomiting, diarrhea, increased thirst, excessive urination, or neurological signs such as tremors after long-term heavy feeding. If your pet ate a large amount of seasoned tuna or shows persistent symptoms, contact your vet.

## Safer Alternatives

For a fish-based treat, consider plain cooked whitefish, sardines in water, or small amounts of cooked salmon. Commercial fish-flavored pet foods are formulated to be complete and balanced, making them a better regular option than human tuna.
