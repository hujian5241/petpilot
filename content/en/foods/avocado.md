# Avocado

Avocado contains persin, which can cause gastrointestinal upset in dogs and cats. The large pit also poses a choking and obstruction risk.

## Two Risks in One Fruit

Avocado presents both a chemical and a physical danger. The chemical risk comes from persin, a natural compound that can irritate the digestive tract. The physical risk comes from the large, smooth pit, which can lodge in the throat or intestines. Both risks make avocado a poor choice for pets.

## Persin and Digestive Upset

Persin is most concentrated in the leaves, skin, and pit, but the flesh also contains smaller amounts. Dogs and cats that eat avocado may develop vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal discomfort. Most cases are mild and self-limiting, but severe or persistent symptoms require veterinary care.

## The Pit Can Be an Emergency

The avocado pit is roughly golf-ball sized and hard. If swallowed whole, it can become stuck in the esophagus, stomach, or intestines. A blockage causes repeated vomiting, loss of appetite, abdominal pain, and constipation. Some obstructions require surgical removal.

## Species Differences in Sensitivity

Dogs and cats are relatively resistant to persin compared with other animals. Birds, rabbits, guinea pigs, horses, and some livestock can develop severe respiratory distress and heart damage from avocado exposure. If you keep multiple types of pets, avocado should be kept far away from all of them.

## Guacamole Is Worse Than Plain Avocado

Guacamole combines avocado with onion, garlic, salt, lime juice, and spices. Each of these ingredients can be harmful or irritating to pets. The high salt and fat content also increase the risk of pancreatitis. Never share guacamole or avocado-based dips with pets.

## When to Call the Vet

If your pet ate a small amount of avocado flesh and shows only mild stomach upset, monitor closely. If vomiting or diarrhea persists, or if your pet swallowed any part of the pit or skin, contact a veterinarian. Early intervention can prevent a minor issue from becoming a surgical emergency.

## Safer Fruits to Share

Blueberries, strawberries, watermelon without seeds, apple slices without seeds, and cucumber are all safer choices. These fruits are lower in fat, free of persin, and unlikely to cause obstruction. Always offer them in small portions.
