# Honey

Honey is safe for most dogs in very small amounts but is high in sugar. It should not be given to diabetic or overweight pets.

Cats can have a tiny taste, but they do not need sweets. Avoid giving honey to kittens.

## Why Honey Matters for Pets

Honey may seem wholesome, but its sugar content is similar to other sweets. Even natural sugar can contribute to obesity, dental disease, and unstable blood sugar. Pets that already have health conditions are especially vulnerable.

## How Honey Affects Dogs and Cats

In dogs, a small lick is usually harmless, but larger amounts may lead to an upset stomach or a rapid rise in blood glucose. Cats are less equipped to handle sugary foods and may develop diarrhea or vomiting. The sticky texture can also coat teeth and feed cavity-causing bacteria.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog licks honey from a spoon, jar, or piece of bread.
- Honey is mixed into homemade treats or used to hide pills.
- A curious pet knocks over a container and consumes several spoonfuls.
- A well-meaning owner offers honey to a tired puppy.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, or increased thirst. Diabetic pets may show weakness, confusion, or excessive urination. Contact your vet if symptoms persist or if a large amount was eaten.

## Safer Alternatives

Instead of honey, offer small pieces of blueberries, apple slices, or carrots. These options provide fiber and moisture without the sugar spike. Always remove seeds and cores, and introduce any new treat slowly.
