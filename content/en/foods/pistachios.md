# Pistachios

Plain pistachios are not toxic to dogs, but the shells can cause choking or intestinal blockage. Salted pistachios can contribute to sodium toxicity and should be avoided.

## Why Pistachios Matter for Pets

Pistachios are a common snack in many households, often served in open bowls during gatherings. Their small size and hard shells make them easy for dogs to grab and swallow whole. Salted, roasted, or flavored varieties add even more concerns, including excess sodium and seasonings that may irritate a pet's stomach.

## How Pistachios Affects Dogs and Cats

The fat content of pistachios can be hard for pets to digest, especially in larger amounts. Dogs that eat many pistachios may develop vomiting, diarrhea, or inflammation of the pancreas. The shells are not digestible and can become lodged in the stomach or intestines, sometimes requiring surgery.

Cats have even less need for high-fat snacks. A tiny taste of plain pistachio meat is unlikely to cause harm, but regular feeding offers no benefit and may lead to digestive upset or weight gain.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog knocks over a bowl and eats pistachios with shells.
- A pet finds salted pistachios left on a coffee table.
- A cat licks flavored residue from empty shells.
- A dog raids the trash for discarded pistachio shells.

## What to Watch For

Signs of trouble include vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, lethargy, or difficulty passing stool. Contact your veterinarian if shells were swallowed or if symptoms last more than a day.

## Safer Alternatives

Carrot sticks, green beans, or a small amount of plain peanut butter without xylitol are better choices for a crunchy or savory treat. These options are lower in fat and free from shell-related risks.
