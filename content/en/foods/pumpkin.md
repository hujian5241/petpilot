# Pumpkin

Plain pumpkin is a well-tolerated source of fiber that can help firm loose stool or relieve mild constipation in dogs and cats. It is low in calories and rich in beta-carotene. Many veterinarians recommend a small amount of pumpkin for digestive support.

## Why Pumpkin Matters for Pets

Pumpkin is one of the most commonly recommended human foods for pets with mild digestive issues. It is affordable, easy to find, and gentle on the stomach. Because it is low in calories, it can also be used as a nutritious food topper for pets on a weight-management plan.

## How Pumpkin Affects Dogs and Cats

The soluble fiber in pumpkin absorbs excess water in the digestive tract, which can help firm loose stool. At the same time, it adds moisture and bulk, which can ease mild constipation. Pumpkin also provides beta-carotene and other nutrients that support overall health.

Most dogs tolerate pumpkin well and may enjoy it as a regular addition to meals. Cats can also benefit, though they need only a small amount. For both species, plain pumpkin is key. Pumpkin pie filling, which contains sugar, spices, and sometimes dairy, should never be fed.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog has loose stool after a sudden food change.
- A cat is constipated or having trouble with hairballs.
- An owner wants a low-calorie way to add fiber to meals.
- Fresh pumpkin is cooked and shared after the holidays.

## What to Watch For

Pumpkin is generally safe, but too much can cause orange stool, gas, or constipation. If digestive symptoms do not improve within a day or two, or if your pet seems painful or lethargic, contact your veterinarian.

## Safer Alternatives

Sweet potato, butternut squash, and carrots offer similar fiber and nutrients. Always serve these plain and cooked, without butter, salt, or seasonings.
