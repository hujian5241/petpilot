# Almonds

Almonds are not toxic to dogs, but they are difficult to digest and can cause gastrointestinal upset. Whole almonds can also pose a choking hazard or cause intestinal blockage, especially in small dogs.

Cats should avoid almonds because their digestive systems are not designed to process nuts. Choose safer treats such as carrots or apple slices instead.

## Why Almonds Matter for Pets

Almonds are often seen as a healthy snack, but they are not a natural food for dogs or cats. Their hard texture and high fat content can irritate the digestive tract. In addition, salted, chocolate-covered, or flavored almonds bring extra risks.

## How Almonds Affect Dogs and Cats

Dogs may swallow almonds whole, which can lead to choking or intestinal blockage. Even when chewed, almonds can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and gas. The high fat content may also trigger pancreatitis in sensitive pets.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog eats almonds from a snack bowl or counter.
- Trail mix containing almonds, raisins, or chocolate is left out.
- Almond butter is offered from a spoon.
- A pet finds almond shells or pieces on the floor.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort, or difficulty passing stool. If your pet ate many almonds or shows signs of blockage such as lethargy or bloating, contact your vet. Chocolate-covered almonds require immediate veterinary advice.

## Safer Alternatives

Choose pet-safe snacks like carrots, apple slices, or a small amount of plain peanut butter. These options are easier to digest and lower in oxalates. Always check labels for added salt, sugar, or xylitol.
