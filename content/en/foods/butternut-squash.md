# Butternut Squash

Butternut squash is a nutritious vegetable that provides fiber, potassium, and vitamin A. Dogs can eat it cooked and mashed or cut into soft cubes. The seeds and hard rind should be removed to prevent choking and digestive blockage.

Cats can have a very small amount of plain cooked squash. Do not feed butternut squash soups or roasted dishes that contain garlic, onions, nutmeg, or salt. Raw squash is too tough and should be avoided.

## Why Butternut Squash Matters for Pets

Butternut squash is a popular addition to homemade dog food and treats because it is low in calories and high in fiber. The main concerns are preparation and portion size: raw pieces, skin, seeds, and seasoned dishes can all cause problems.

## How Butternut Squash Affects Dogs and Cats

When cooked, butternut squash is gentle on the stomach and easy to absorb. Its fiber can help regulate bowel movements, and vitamin A supports vision and immune health. Because it is starchy, it can raise blood sugar, so diabetic pets should receive only small amounts. The hard rind and seeds may cause blockage if swallowed.

## Common Scenarios

- Mixing plain mashed squash into your dog's kibble
- Roasting squash with garlic and herbs for human dinner
- Letting your pet chew on a raw squash piece
- Sharing a spoonful of seasoned butternut squash soup

## What to Watch For

Most pets tolerate plain cooked butternut squash well. Watch for gas, bloating, or loose stool after new servings. If your pet ate raw squash, the skin, or a seasoned dish, monitor for vomiting or discomfort and contact your vet if symptoms persist.

## Safer Alternatives

Pumpkin, sweet potato, and carrots offer similar nutritional benefits. Plain canned pumpkin is a convenient fiber option. For cats, skip the vegetables and offer a small piece of cooked lean meat instead.
