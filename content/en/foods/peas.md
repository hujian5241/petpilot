# Peas

Peas are a safe vegetable for dogs and are commonly found in commercial dog foods. They provide plant protein, fiber, and several vitamins. Fresh or frozen plain peas are best. Avoid canned peas, which may be high in sodium.

## Why Peas Matter for Pets

Peas are a familiar vegetable that many pets encounter, both in human meals and in commercial pet food. Their small size and mild sweetness make them an easy treat to offer by hand. Because they appear in many dishes, it is important to know which preparations are safe.

## How Peas Affects Dogs and Cats

Plain peas are a good source of plant protein, fiber, vitamin A, vitamin K, and several B vitamins. Dogs usually digest them well in moderation. However, peas can cause gas in some dogs, especially when given in large amounts.

Cats can eat a few plain peas, but they are obligate carnivores and do not need plant protein. Their main nutrition should come from meat-based foods.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog eats fresh peas from the garden.
- Frozen peas are used as a low-calorie training reward.
- A pet gets into a pea side dish made with butter and salt.
- A pet with kidney disease is fed peas regularly.

## What to Watch For

Watch for gas, bloating, or diarrhea. Canned peas and pea dishes from human meals may contain high sodium or harmful ingredients like garlic and onions. Pets with kidney disease may need to avoid peas due to phosphorus content.

## Safer Alternatives

Green beans, carrots, and small pieces of sweet potato are good vegetable alternatives. As with any treat, keep portions small and avoid seasoned preparations.
