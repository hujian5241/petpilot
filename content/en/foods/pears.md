# Pears

Pears are safe for dogs when seeds and core are removed. They provide fiber and vitamins.

## Why Pears Matter for Pets

Pears are a convenient, sweet fruit that many dogs enjoy. They are lower in acid than citrus fruits and generally easy on the stomach when given in small amounts. The fiber in pears can support digestive health, but the seeds and core must be removed before sharing.

## How Pears Affect Dogs and Cats

Ripe pear flesh is soft, juicy, and easy to chew. The natural sugar provides energy but can cause diarrhea if overfed. The seeds and core are the main concern because they contain compounds that can release cyanide when chewed. The core can also be a choking hazard, especially for small dogs.

## Common Scenarios

A safe scenario is a dog receiving a few small slices of pear with seeds and core removed. A riskier scenario is a dog eating a whole pear from a counter or tree, including the seeds. Canned pears in syrup add unnecessary sugar, and pear cores left in compost or trash can attract curious pets.

## What to Watch For

After feeding pear, watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or signs of choking. If your pet swallowed a large piece of core or many seeds, contact your veterinarian. Pets with diabetes, obesity, or inflammatory bowel disease should only have pears on a vet's advice.

## Safer Alternatives

Apple slices without seeds and blueberries are good fruit alternatives for dogs. For cats, small pieces of plain cooked meat or commercial treats are better than fruit. If you want a high-fiber vegetable for a dog, plain cooked pumpkin or green beans are usually more reliable choices.
