# Corn

Plain cooked corn kernels are safe for most dogs in moderation. Corn is a common carbohydrate in pet foods. The greatest danger is the cob, which can cause life-threatening intestinal obstruction if swallowed.

Cats can have a few plain kernels. Avoid buttered corn, corn on the cob, and corn dishes with salt or spices. Some pets may be allergic to corn; discontinue if you notice itching or digestive issues.

## Why Corn Matters for Pets

Corn is a familiar grain that appears in many commercial pet foods as a source of carbohydrates. When served plainly and in small portions, it can be an acceptable treat for dogs. Pet owners should focus on preparation and portion size to keep corn safe.

## How Corn Affects Dogs and Cats

Most dogs digest cooked corn kernels without problems when they are given in moderation. The starchy content means large amounts can add calories and may upset the stomach. Cats can tolerate a few kernels, though their diet should be primarily animal protein.

## Common Scenarios

- A few plain kernels fall on the floor during dinner.
- A dog snatches a corn cob from a picnic plate.
- A pet eats seasoned corn or cornbread with added ingredients.
- Tortilla chips are left within reach of a curious dog.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or signs of an allergic reaction such as itching or ear infections. If a corn cob was swallowed, contact your vet immediately even if your pet seems fine at first. Blockages can become serious quickly.

## Safer Alternatives

Green beans, carrots, or small pieces of cooked sweet potato offer crunch and nutrients with fewer risks. These vegetables are lower in calories and do not present a choking or blockage hazard.
