# Salmon

Plain, cooked salmon is a healthy protein source rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which support skin, coat, and joint health. It should always be fully cooked and deboned before feeding.

Raw salmon, especially from the Pacific Northwest, can carry a parasite that causes salmon poisoning disease, which can be fatal. Smoked salmon is also too salty for pets.

## Why Salmon Matters for Pets

Salmon is widely praised as a nutritious food, and many pet foods include it for omega-3 benefits. However, home-prepared salmon comes with preparation rules. The risk of salmon poisoning disease and high sodium in smoked products means owners should be cautious about how salmon is shared with pets.

## How Salmon Affects Dogs and Cats

Cooked salmon is generally safe and provides high-quality protein along with fatty acids that may reduce inflammation and improve coat condition. Raw salmon is the main concern, particularly for dogs, because it can carry a parasite that leads to fever, vomiting, diarrhea, dehydration, and weakness. Cats can also be affected by raw fish and should never be fed raw salmon.

## Common Scenarios

- **Plain cooked fillet:** A small, boneless piece is usually safe for dogs and cats.
- **Raw salmon:** Contact a vet immediately; do not wait for symptoms.
- **Smoked or cured salmon:** Too salty; avoid sharing.
- **Salmon with seasoning or sauce:** Garlic, onions, and rich sauces are unsafe.
- **Salmon skin:** High in fat and best removed before feeding.

## What to Watch For

After raw salmon ingestion, watch for fever, vomiting, diarrhea, weakness, swollen lymph nodes, or loss of appetite. These signs may appear days later. For cooked salmon, mild vomiting or diarrhea may occur if your pet is sensitive to rich fish or if bones were swallowed.

## Safer Alternatives

If you want the benefits of fish without the raw-salmon risk, consider small amounts of plain cooked whitefish, sardines packed in water, or commercially prepared fish-based pet foods that are nutritionally balanced. Eggs are another protein option for many pets.
