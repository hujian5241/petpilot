# Cashews

Cashews are generally safe for dogs when plain and unsalted, but they are high in fat. Too many can cause stomach upset or contribute to pancreatitis. Salted or flavored cashews should be avoided.

Cats do not need nuts in their diet, and cashews can cause digestive upset. Keep portion sizes very small if offered at all.

## Why Cashews Matter for Pets

Cashews are one of the softer tree nuts, which can make them seem like a safer choice. However, their high fat and calorie content means they should still be considered an occasional treat at most. Salted, honey-roasted, or chocolate-covered cashews introduce extra dangers.

## How Cashews Affect Dogs and Cats

Plain cashews in small amounts are usually tolerated by dogs, but larger portions can cause vomiting or diarrhea. The fat load can also trigger pancreatitis in susceptible pets. Cats are less able to process nuts and may develop stomach upset even from small amounts.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog eats a few cashews from a snack mix or bowl.
- A bag of salted cashews is left within reach.
- Cashew butter is spread on a treat or licked from a spoon.
- Flavored or party mix cashews are dropped on the floor.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, gas, lethargy, or loss of appetite. If your pet shows signs of pancreatitis such as abdominal pain or repeated vomiting, contact your vet. Seek advice immediately if chocolate-covered or xylitol-containing cashews were eaten.

## Safer Alternatives

Offer carrots, green beans, or apple slices for a crunchy treat. If you want a nutty flavor, a tiny amount of plain, xylitol-free peanut butter is a better option. Always avoid flavored or salted varieties.
