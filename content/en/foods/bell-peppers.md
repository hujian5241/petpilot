# Bell Peppers

Bell peppers, especially red ones, are safe for dogs and provide vitamin C and antioxidants. They are crunchy and low in calories. Remove the stem and seeds before feeding, as they can be difficult to digest.

## Why Bell Peppers Matter for Pets

Bell peppers are a colorful, crunchy vegetable that many people keep on hand for salads, stir-fries, and snacks. Their bright appearance can attract curious pets. Because they are not spicy, they are very different from hot peppers and can be a safe treat when prepared correctly.

## How Bell Peppers Affects Dogs and Cats

Bell peppers contain vitamin C, vitamin A, antioxidants, and fiber. Red bell peppers are the ripest and typically contain the most nutrients. Dogs can eat raw or cooked bell peppers in small amounts, though some dogs may prefer the softer texture of cooked peppers.

Cats can have a tiny taste, but most are not interested. Bell peppers do not provide nutrients that cats cannot get from their regular food.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog begs for a strip of raw bell pepper while you are chopping vegetables.
- A few pieces fall on the floor during meal prep.
- A pet owner wants a colorful, low-calorie training reward.
- A dog eats peppers from a seasoned stir-fry.

## What to Watch For

Always remove the stem and seeds, as they can be tough to digest. Never confuse bell peppers with hot peppers, chili peppers, or dishes containing onion and garlic. Seasonings and oils can upset your pet's stomach.

## Safer Alternatives

Carrots, cucumber, and green beans are similar crunchy, low-calorie treats. These options are widely tolerated and easy to prepare.
