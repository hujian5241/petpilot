# Pumpkin Seeds

Plain roasted pumpkin seeds are generally safe for dogs and can be a crunchy source of fiber and minerals. Avoid salted or seasoned seeds.

## Why Pumpkin Seeds Matter for Pets

Pumpkin seeds, also called pepitas, are often saved after pumpkin carving or fall baking. Their crunchy texture and mild flavor make them tempting to share with pets. While they are not toxic, preparation and portion size are important.

## How Pumpkin Seeds Affects Dogs and Cats

Roasted pumpkin seeds contain fiber, magnesium, zinc, and healthy fats. In small amounts, they can be a nutritious treat for dogs. However, their fat and calorie content mean they should be limited, especially for pets with pancreatitis, obesity, or sensitive digestion.

Salted or flavored pumpkin seeds can cause stomach upset and add unnecessary sodium. Raw shells may be difficult to digest and could irritate the gastrointestinal tract. Grinding seeds into a powder can make them easier for some pets to tolerate.

Cats can have a few plain seeds, but seeds are not a necessary part of their diet. Most cats will do fine without them.

## Common Scenarios

- Leftover roasted seeds from a holiday pumpkin.
- A dog gets into a seasoned snack mix containing pumpkin seeds.
- An owner wants to add fiber using fresh pumpkin seeds.
- A cat bats around and eats a few loose seeds.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or loss of appetite. If your pet ate a large amount of salted seeds or shows signs of blockage, contact your veterinarian.

## Safer Alternatives

Carrots, sweet potato chunks, and plain cooked pumpkin provide similar benefits without the high fat and shell-related concerns. These are better choices for regular treating.
