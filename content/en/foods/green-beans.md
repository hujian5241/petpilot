# Green Beans

Green beans are a healthy, low-calorie vegetable that can be fed to dogs either raw or cooked. They are often recommended by veterinarians as a filler treat for overweight dogs because they provide bulk without many calories. Cats can eat a small amount, but they receive little nutritional benefit.

## Why Green Beans Matter for Pets

Green beans are one of the easiest vegetables to share with pets. They are widely available, inexpensive, and can be served raw, steamed, or frozen. Their crunch and mild flavor make them appealing to many dogs, and their low calorie count makes them ideal for pets watching their weight.

## How Green Beans Affects Dogs and Cats

The fiber and water content in green beans can help dogs feel full without adding many calories. This makes green beans a popular choice for weight management when used under veterinary guidance. They also provide vitamins such as vitamin K, vitamin C, and manganese.

Cats do not require vegetables, but a few small pieces of plain green beans are unlikely to cause harm. Most cats will simply ignore them.

## Common Scenarios

- A dog on a weight-loss plan receives green beans as a snack.
- Frozen green beans are offered as a refreshing summer treat.
- A pet owner shares plain steamed green beans from dinner.
- A dog accidentally eats green beans from a garlicky stir-fry.

## What to Watch For

Plain green beans are safe, but those cooked with garlic, onions, butter, or salt can be harmful. Too many green beans can cause gas, bloating, or diarrhea. Introduce them gradually and keep treats to less than 10 percent of daily calories.

## Safer Alternatives

Carrots, cucumber slices, and steamed broccoli are other low-calorie options. Always serve vegetables plain and in appropriate portions for your pet's size.
