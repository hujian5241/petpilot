# Blueberries

Blueberries are widely considered safe for dogs and can be a healthy, low-calorie treat. They contain antioxidants, fiber, and vitamins.

## Why Blueberries Matter for Pets

Blueberries are small, convenient, and packed with antioxidants, which makes them a favorite treat for many pet owners. They are low enough in calories to use as training rewards without quickly unbalancing a diet. When served plain and in moderation, they can add variety and enrichment.

## How Blueberries Affect Dogs and Cats

Dogs usually digest blueberries well and can benefit from the fiber and vitamins in small amounts. Because they are naturally sweet, portion control is still important. Cats do not need fruit, but a single berry given as a curiosity is unlikely to cause harm.

## Common Scenarios

- Using blueberries during a training session.
- Adding a few berries on top of a regular meal.
- A pet finds a dropped blueberry in the kitchen.
- Frozen berries are offered as a cool snack.

## What to Watch For

Mild diarrhea or soft stool can occur if a pet eats too many blueberries. Watch for any signs of digestive upset, and reduce the amount next time. Make sure blueberries are not mistaken for grapes, which are dangerous for dogs.

## Safer Alternatives

Strawberries, raspberries, or apple slices without seeds are good alternatives. These fruits are also low in calories and can be served in small, controlled portions.
