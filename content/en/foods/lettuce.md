# Lettuce

Lettuce is safe for dogs but provides minimal nutrition. It is mostly water and fiber. Large amounts can cause diarrhea. Serve plain lettuce without salad dressing, vinegar, onions, or croutons.

Cats can eat a small piece of lettuce. It is not harmful but is not a meaningful addition to their diet. Romaine lettuce is preferable to iceberg, which is the least nutritious variety.

## Why Lettuce Matters for Pets

Lettuce can be a useful low-calorie treat for pets that enjoy crunching on greens. It adds moisture and bulk without adding many calories, which can help satisfy a begging dog. However, it should not replace more nutrient-dense treats or regular meals.

## How Lettuce Affects Dogs and Cats

In small amounts, lettuce passes through the digestive system easily and may add a little hydration. Because it is so high in water and fiber, too much lettuce can loosen stool or cause diarrhea. Cats gain little from lettuce because their bodies are designed to process animal protein.

## Common Scenarios

- A leaf falls on the floor while preparing a salad.
- A dog begs for a bite of a plain sandwich with lettuce.
- A pet licks ranch dressing off a lettuce leaf.
- Someone offers a large handful of lettuce as a snack.

## What to Watch For

Watch for loose stool, increased bowel movements, or mild stomach upset after a large serving. Salad toppings such as onions, croutons, and dressings can be harmful and should never be fed. If diarrhea lasts more than a day, contact your vet.

## Safer Alternatives

Cucumber slices, green beans, or carrot pieces offer more crunch and nutrients. They are also low in calories and less likely to cause diarrhea when fed in moderation.
