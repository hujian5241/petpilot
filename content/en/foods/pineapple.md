# Pineapple

Fresh pineapple is safe for dogs in small amounts. Avoid canned pineapple in syrup.

## Why Pineapple Matters for Pets

Pineapple is a refreshing fruit that can feel like a special treat on a hot day. For dogs, a few chunks of fresh pineapple can be a safe and hydrating snack. The key is to prepare it correctly and keep portions small. Canned pineapple and the tough parts of the fruit can cause problems.

## How Pineapple Affects Dogs and Cats

The soft flesh of pineapple is easy for most dogs to digest in small amounts. The natural sugar provides quick energy, but too much can lead to diarrhea or an upset stomach. The acidity of pineapple may bother some pets, especially those with sensitive stomachs. The core, skin, and crown are too tough to digest and should always be removed.

## Common Scenarios

A dog may enjoy a small piece of fresh pineapple as a reward. Issues arise when owners offer large amounts, canned pineapple in syrup, or the fibrous core. Some cats may show curiosity, but fruit is not a natural part of their diet. If a dog develops loose stools after pineapple, reduce the portion or skip it altogether.

## What to Watch For

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, or excessive drooling after feeding pineapple. If your pet chewed on the core or skin, monitor for signs of blockage such as repeated vomiting, loss of appetite, or abdominal pain. Pets with diabetes, obesity, or dental disease should have pineapple only rarely and in very small amounts.

## Safer Alternatives

Blueberries, small pieces of banana, and seedless watermelon are lower-risk fruit choices for dogs. For cats, stick to meat-based treats. If you are looking for a hydrating snack, cucumber slices or small amounts of plain cooked squash can be good options for dogs.
