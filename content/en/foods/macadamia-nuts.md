# Macadamia Nuts

Macadamia nuts are known to be toxic to dogs, causing weakness, vomiting, tremors, and elevated body temperature. The exact toxic mechanism is unknown, but symptoms usually appear within 12 hours.

## A Unique Canine Toxin

Among nuts, macadamias stand out because they are specifically toxic to dogs. While many nuts are unsafe due to high fat or salt, macadamias cause a distinct neurological reaction. Dogs may become weak, wobbly, and unable to rise normally. Fortunately, most recover fully with supportive care.

## What Owners Typically See

After eating macadamia nuts, a dog may vomit and become lethargic. The hallmark sign is weakness, especially in the hind legs. Some dogs develop tremors or a low-grade fever. These signs can be alarming, but they usually resolve within a day or two with treatment.

## Small Amounts Can Cause Signs

Toxicity has been reported at less than one gram of nuts per pound of body weight. For a small dog, just two or three nuts may be enough to cause weakness. Larger dogs may need to eat more, but the risk is still real.

## Often Combined With Other Hazards

Macadamia nuts rarely appear alone. They are common in white chocolate macadamia cookies, trail mix, candy, and baked goods. These combinations add chocolate, raisins, xylitol, or excessive fat to the danger, making prompt veterinary advice even more important.

## Treatment Is Supportive

There is no antidote, so treatment focuses on symptoms. The vet may induce vomiting if the ingestion was recent and provide intravenous fluids, anti-nausea medication, and temperature support. Most dogs improve quickly and return to normal within 48 hours.

## Safer Nut and Treat Options

If you want to offer a crunchy treat, choose small pieces of carrot, apple, or cucumber. Plain, unsalted peanut butter is a popular alternative, but check the label for xylitol. Avoid all mixed nuts and keep baking ingredients stored securely.
