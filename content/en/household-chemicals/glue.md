safety:
  dogs:
    status: limited
    severity: moderate
    summary: >-
      Most household glues are low in systemic toxicity, but they can adhere to
      skin and fur, irritate the mouth and stomach, and form solid masses that
      may cause gastrointestinal obstruction.
  cats:
    status: limited
    severity: moderate
    summary: >-
      Cats are at risk from grooming adhesive off their fur, which can lead to
      oral irritation, pawing at the mouth, vomiting, and potential intestinal
      blockage.
symptoms:
  - drooling
  - pawing at the mouth
  - gagging
  - vomiting
  - loss of appetite
  - lethargy
  - abdominal pain
  - constipation
  - difficulty breathing if fumes inhaled
what_to_do: >-
  Do not pull glued fur or skin apart. Rinse the area gently with warm water,
  trim hair if possible, and contact a veterinarian. If glue was swallowed, call
  your vet or a pet poison control center; do not induce vomiting.
