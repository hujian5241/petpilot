Fabric softeners and dryer sheets are common laundry products that can be toxic to both dogs and cats. The primary concern is cationic surfactants, also known as quaternary ammonium compounds, which are irritating to mucous membranes and can cause drooling, vomiting, oral ulcers, and gastrointestinal distress. In severe cases, especially if liquid softener is aspirated into the lungs during vomiting, pets can develop aspiration pneumonia.

Exposure usually happens when pets chew on dryer sheets, lick spilled liquid fabric softener, or groom fabric that still has heavy residue. Concentrated liquid softener is particularly dangerous because even a small amount can burn the mouth and esophagus. Dryer sheets may be appealing because of their texture and fragrance, but they contain the same active chemicals.
