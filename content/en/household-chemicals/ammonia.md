Ammonia is a powerful, caustic cleaning agent found in many glass cleaners, floor cleaners, and multipurpose sprays. While it cuts grease effectively, it releases strong fumes that can irritate a pet’s eyes, nose, throat, and lungs. Dogs and cats are especially vulnerable because they live close to the floor where residues settle, and their respiratory systems are more sensitive than those of humans.

Exposure usually happens when pets walk across freshly cleaned floors and lick residue from their paws, inhale fumes from recently sprayed surfaces, or come into direct contact with the liquid. Inhalation may cause coughing, sneezing, tearing, and difficulty breathing, while skin or eye contact can lead to redness, burns, and excessive drooling. Ingestion of concentrated ammonia can damage the mouth, throat, and stomach.

Prevention is simple: keep pets out of the room during cleaning and until all surfaces are fully dry, ensure good ventilation, store products in a secure cabinet, and never mix ammonia with bleach, which produces toxic chloramine gas. Whenever possible, choose pet-safe cleaners, and rinse floors with clean water after using ammonia-based products to reduce leftover residue.
