Potpourri may seem like a harmless way to freshen your home, but it poses serious risks to both dogs and cats. Simmering potpourri pots contain hot, caustic liquid that can cause severe burns to the mouth, tongue, and esophagus if a curious pet laps from the bowl or spills it onto themselves. Dried potpourri and oil-based formulations are often scented with concentrated essential oils and fragrance compounds that can be toxic when inhaled, licked, or absorbed through the skin.

Cats are particularly vulnerable to essential oil poisoning because they lack certain liver enzymes needed to process these compounds. Even small exposures can lead to drooling, vomiting, difficulty breathing, tremors, weakness, and in severe cases, liver damage. Dogs can also develop gastrointestinal upset, central nervous system depression, and skin irritation from contact with potpourri oils or heavily scented products.

Exposure typically happens when a pet drinks from a simmering pot, chews on dried potpourri, rubs against oil diffusers, or walks through spilled liquid. Because potpourri is often left unattended in warmers or open bowls, pets can access it without supervision. Birds and small animals are also highly sensitive to airborne fragrance chemicals, so caution is needed throughout the home.

To protect your pets, keep potpourri, simmering pots, and oil diffusers in areas your animals cannot reach. Consider using safer alternatives such as pet-safe air fresheners, baking soda odor absorbers, or fresh air ventilation. If you suspect your pet has ingested or come into contact with potpourri, contact your veterinarian or a pet poison control center immediately.
