**Warfarin is a prescription blood thinner used in humans and is extremely dangerous to dogs and cats.** Even a small amount can interfere with blood clotting and cause internal bleeding that may not be obvious for hours or even days. Because pets are much smaller than people and metabolize medications differently, a dose that seems minor can be life-threatening.

Exposure usually happens when a pet eats a dropped pill, chews through a medication bottle, or consumes food containing warfarin. Dogs are especially at risk because they may swallow entire pill vials or tablets found on counters, nightstands, or in bags. Cats can also be poisoned if they lick up spilled tablet fragments or powder, or if they are given human medication by mistake.

Early signs of warfarin poisoning can be subtle and include weakness, lethargy, pale gums, and bruising. More serious symptoms include coughing blood, bloody urine or stool, bleeding gums, collapse, and difficulty breathing. Because bleeding may be internal and hidden, any suspected ingestion should be treated as an emergency.

Keep all warfarin and other human medications in a closed cabinet well out of reach. Never give your pet any human blood thinner, and clean up dropped pills immediately. If your pet needs anticoagulant therapy, ask your veterinarian about safe, veterinary-approved alternatives.
