safety:
  dogs:
    status: toxic
    severity: high
    summary: >-
      Pseudoephedrine is a potent stimulant in dogs and can cause hyperactivity,
      tremors, seizures, dangerous heart rhythm abnormalities, elevated blood
      pressure, and collapse. Even a single tablet or small amount of liquid can
      be life-threatening.
  cats:
    status: toxic
    severity: critical
    summary: >-
      Cats are extremely sensitive to pseudoephedrine. Small exposures can
      trigger severe agitation, tremors, seizures, hyperthermia, and
      cardiovascular collapse. Pseudoephedrine should be considered a medical
      emergency in cats.
