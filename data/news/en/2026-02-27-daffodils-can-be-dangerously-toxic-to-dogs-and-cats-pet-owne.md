## What happened
Derry Journal warned on 27 February 2026 that daffodils can be dangerously toxic to dogs and cats, alerting owners as the flowers come into season.

## Key facts
- Affected animals: dogs and cats.
- Daffodils contain lycorine and other alkaloids; bulbs are especially toxic.
- Ingestion can cause vomiting, drooling, abdominal pain, and heart rhythm issues.

## What pet owners should do
Keep daffodils and bulbs out of reach, avoid planting them where pets dig, and call a vet immediately if any part of the plant is eaten.
