## What happened
Encyclopedia Britannica published an article on June 24, 2026, explaining why lilies are toxic to cats and what makes the plant dangerous.

## Key facts
- Affected animals: cats
- All parts of the lily plant—including pollen, petals, and leaves—can be toxic
- Ingestion can cause severe kidney failure in cats
- Source: Encyclopedia Britannica, published June 24, 2026

## What pet owners should do
Keep lilies out of homes and gardens with cats. If a cat chews a leaf, gets pollen on its fur, or shows vomiting, lethargy, or loss of appetite, seek emergency veterinary care immediately.
