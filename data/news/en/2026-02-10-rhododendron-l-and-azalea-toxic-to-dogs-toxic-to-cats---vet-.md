## What happened
Vet Times reported on 10 February 2026 that rhododendrons and azaleas (Rhododendron L.) are toxic to dogs and cats, warning owners to keep pets away from these common garden shrubs.

## Key facts
- Affected animals: dogs and cats.
- These plants contain grayanotoxins that can affect the heart, nerves, and digestive system.
- Ingestion can be life-threatening.

## What pet owners should do
Prevent access to rhododendrons and azaleas, remove fallen leaves or flowers, and contact a vet immediately if your pet chews any part of the plant.
