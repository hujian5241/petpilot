Revival Animal Health expanded its nationwide recall of canine milk replacers on June 25 to include Breeder’s Edge Foster Care GM (goat milk) products, after testing confirmed elevated vitamin D levels and two reports of rickets. The recall was originally issued April 17 for Breeder’s Edge Foster Care Canine and Shelter’s Choice Canine Milk Replacers because of variable vitamin D levels, which can mean too much or too little of the nutrient. High vitamin D can cause vomiting, diarrhea, weakness, lethargy, weight loss, and kidney calcification; low levels in puppies can cause rickets, bone deformities, and lameness. The products were sold online and in retail pet stores nationwide. Owners should stop using the affected products and contact Revival Animal Health at 1-877-870-4059 for a full refund.

**Brand/Company:** Revival Animal Health

**Product:** Breeder’s Edge Foster Care GM (goat milk), Breeder’s Edge Foster Care Canine, and Shelter’s Choice Canine Milk Replacers

**Affected animals:** dogs

**Reason:** Variable vitamin D levels, with some products containing too much vitamin D and others too little

**Lot / UPC / Best by:** Breeder’s Edge Foster Care GM — 1 lb., 5 lb.; Shelter’s Choice Canine Milk Replacer — 1 lb., 3.5 lb., 8 lb.; Breeder’s Edge Foster Care Canine Milk Replacer — 12 oz., 4.5 lb., 18 lb., 20 lb. A full list of affected UPC codes and best-by date ranges is available via the FDA link.

**Location:** Nationwide (United States)

**Recall contact / what to do:** Owners should stop using the affected products and contact Revival Animal Health at 1-877-870-4059 for a full refund.
