## What happened
MSN published a warning on Feb. 26, 2026, about the dangers xylitol poses to pets. The report highlights xylitol as a common ingredient in gum and other products.

## Key facts
Xylitol is a sugar substitute that can trigger insulin release and hypoglycemia in animals. Ingestion may lead to lethargy, seizures, and liver damage. The article flagged "other" animals as affected.

## What pet owners should do
Keep gum, candy, and other xylitol-sweetened items away from pets. Check ingredient labels, especially on sugar-free products. If ingestion occurs, contact a veterinarian or pet poison helpline immediately.
