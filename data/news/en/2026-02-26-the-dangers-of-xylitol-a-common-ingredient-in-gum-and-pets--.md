## What happened
MSN reported on the dangers that xylitol poses to pets, noting that it is a common ingredient in gum and other products.

## Key facts
- Xylitol is a sugar substitute found in many household items.
- Pets, including cats and other animals, can be affected.
- Ingestion may cause hypoglycemia, seizures, liver failure, or death.
- The original report was published on February 26, 2026.

## What pet owners should do
Keep gum, candy, toothpaste, medications, and other xylitol-containing items away from pets. If you suspect ingestion, contact a veterinarian or pet poison helpline immediately.
