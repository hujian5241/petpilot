## What happened
A Japanese research report found that a wide range of dog and cat food sold in Japan contains toxic PFAS, raising concerns about everyday exposure to persistent industrial chemicals.

## Key facts
- Source: 毎日新聞 (Mainichi Shimbun)
- Affected animals: dogs and cats
- Location: Japan
- PFAS are “forever chemicals” that can accumulate in pets and may pose long-term health risks.

## What pet owners should do
Check ingredient and sourcing information, stay alert for recalls, and talk to a veterinarian if your pet shows unexplained illness or you are worried about chronic exposure.
