## What happened
A Japanese research study found that a wide range of dog and cat food sold in Japan contains toxic PFAS, persistent chemicals often called 'forever chemicals.'

## Key facts
- PFAS are linked to long-term health risks in animals and humans.
- Both dog and cat food products were affected.
- Contamination may come from ingredients, packaging, or processing.

## What pet owners should do
- Watch for recalls or regulatory guidance from Japanese authorities.
- Consider PFAS-free or minimally processed foods when available.
- Consult a veterinarian if a pet shows signs of illness.
