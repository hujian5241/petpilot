## What happened
Pet Poison Helpline published a March 20, 2026 advisory warning that Easter baskets and holiday decorations can pose poisoning hazards to pets.

## Key facts
Lilies, chocolate, xylitol, candy, and Easter grass are among the common seasonal dangers. Cats are especially vulnerable to lilies, while dogs can be sickened by chocolate and xylitol.

## What pet owners should do
Keep Easter baskets, flowers, and candy out of reach; use pet-safe decorations; and contact a veterinarian or poison hotline immediately if a pet ingests a suspected toxin.
